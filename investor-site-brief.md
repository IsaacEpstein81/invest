### 2. Problem

**Headline**

Behavioral change is everywhere. There is no system that learns from it.

**Body**

Across organizations, the work of helping people change is constant, but the intelligence generated by that work disappears. Training stays static. Resources remain fragmented. Behavior between interactions is largely invisible. What works rarely compounds across teams, workflows, or institutions.

**Problem Points**

- Training is static and not tied to outcomes
- Resources are fragmented and not reusable
- Client behavior is largely invisible between sessions
- What works does not compound across organizations

### 3. What We Do

**Headline**

We turn real-world behavior into structured intelligence.

**Body**

We deploy software that captures signals from real workflows, structures them into usable intelligence, and improves the systems around them over time.

**Starting Wedges**

- Training: simulation and readiness
- Resource systems: governed and searchable knowledge
- Client engagement: continuous support between sessions

### 4. Why It Compounds

**Headline**

Every interaction makes the system smarter.

**Body**

OpenRecovery connects training to performance, resources to usage and outcomes, and engagement to action and follow-through. As more behavior moves through the system, the quality of recommendations improves, earlier signals emerge, and outcomes get better over time.

**Compounding Loops**

- Training -> performance
- Resources -> usage -> outcomes
- Engagement -> actions -> follow-through

**Result**

- Better recommendations
- Earlier signals
- Improved outcomes over time

## Visual Direction

### Brand Feel

- Premium
- Intelligent
- Controlled
- Strategic
- Cinematic without feeling theatrical

### Visual Metaphor

Use the idea of raw signals becoming structure. The site should feel like invisible behavioral data is being captured, organized, and turned into a living intelligence system.

### Design Principles

- Avoid generic healthcare visuals
- Avoid generic AI neon clichés
- Use strong typography and spacious composition
- Let motion explain the business model
- Keep each section visually distinct while preserving one coherent visual language

### Suggested Motion

- Hero: ambient field of moving signals resolving into a system
- Problem: fragmented cards or nodes that fail to connect
- What We Do: signals entering three operational wedges
- Why It Compounds: a loop that visibly strengthens over time
- Proof: restrained number reveals and logo choreography
- Opportunity: the system expanding outward into adjacent sectors
