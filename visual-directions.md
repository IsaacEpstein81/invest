# OpenRecovery Visual Directions

## Source Rule

These directions are designed to express the messaging in `Investment Pitch Sites.docx`.
Any images, diagrams, screenshots, metrics, or logos from `Tradtional.pdf` should support the chosen direction without changing the framing.

## Direction 1: Signal To Structure

### Feel

Premium, controlled, intelligent, infrastructural.

### Why It Fits

This direction best expresses the core idea that OpenRecovery captures messy real-world signals and turns them into structured intelligence. It feels category-defining and investor-ready without looking like a generic AI startup.

### Visual System

- Deep graphite or obsidian background
- Warm ivory or soft stone typography
- Signal colors in restrained teal, amber, and muted electric blue
- Thin grids, flowing nodes, and structured motion paths
- Large editorial type paired with clean system text

### Motion

- Ambient signal particles moving through the hero
- Fragmented dots resolving into a coherent system
- Loops strengthening as the user scrolls
- Numbers revealing with confidence, not speed

### Best Use

If we want the site to feel strategic, sophisticated, and built for serious investors.

## Direction 2: Human Systems

### Feel

Warm, precise, modern, outcome-focused.

### Why It Fits

This direction keeps the story grounded in human change rather than abstract AI. It is useful if we want to feel more category-expansive and less like infrastructure software at first glance.

### Visual System

- Bone, sand, ink, and muted forest tones
- Subtle gradients and soft texture instead of hard-gloss interfaces
- Typography that feels editorial and human, not technical
- Diagram language that mixes structure with organic flow

### Motion

- Gentle reveal sequences
- Expanding connection lines between systems
- Text and visual layers that build meaning progressively
- Soft parallax and measured scroll transitions

### Best Use

If we want to emphasize the mission and category vision while still feeling premium.

## Direction 3: Strategic Control

### Feel

Confident, sharp, institutional, high-stakes.

### Why It Fits

This direction leans into the "providers need infrastructure they control" message. It makes OpenRecovery feel like the sovereign intelligence layer in a market full of generic tools.

### Visual System

- Dark navy, iron, off-white, and restrained copper accents
- Strong section dividers and high contrast layouts
- Bold headlines with disciplined spacing
- Diagram motifs inspired by command centers, systems maps, and control layers

### Motion

- Crisp sectional transitions
- Panels locking into place as the system story becomes clearer
- Controlled chart and metric reveals
- Minimal but strong hover states and scroll cues

### Best Use

If we want to foreground defensibility, strategic timing, and market control.

## Recommendation

The best fit is **Signal To Structure**.

It aligns most directly with the DOCX framing:

- captures behavior
- structures intelligence
- improves systems over time
- creates a compounding loop

It also gives us the strongest foundation for impressive motion without drifting into AI clichés or generic healthcare design.

## Visual Usage Rules For The PDF

Use the PDF for:

- verified logos
- verified metrics
- product screenshots
- benchmark or appendix visuals
- system diagrams worth redesigning

Do not use the PDF to dictate:

- the homepage hierarchy
- the opening message
- the tone of the visual system
- the section sequence

## Recommendation For Build

If we move into design and code next, I would build the site in the `Signal To Structure` direction and keep a secondary diligence layer for deeper PDF-based proof.
