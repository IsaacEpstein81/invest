## 2. Problem

**Eyebrow**

The Problem

**Headline**

Behavioral change is everywhere. There is no system that learns from it.

**Body**

Organizations spend enormous effort helping people change, but the intelligence generated by that work rarely compounds. Training stays static. Resources remain scattered. Behavior between interactions is largely invisible. What works in one team, program, or institution is almost never captured in a way that improves the next one.

**Problem Cards**

**Static training**

Training is often passive, inconsistent, and disconnected from real outcomes.

**Fragmented resources**

Knowledge lives across documents, teams, and tools, making it hard to govern, reuse, or improve.

**Invisible behavior**

What happens between sessions, check-ins, or interventions is often the most important signal and the least visible.

**No compounding system**

Even when organizations learn what works, that learning rarely becomes infrastructure.

## 3. What We Do

**Eyebrow**

What We Do

**Headline**

We turn real-world behavior into structured intelligence.

**Body**

OpenRecovery deploys software inside live workflows, where behavior is already happening. We capture signals from training, resources, engagement, and outcomes, then structure those signals into systems that get more useful over time.

**Three Starting Wedges**

**Training**

Improve simulation, readiness, and performance in high-stakes environments.

**Resource systems**

Structure and govern institutional knowledge so it can be searched, trusted, and applied.

**Client engagement**

Extend support between formal interactions and create visibility into follow-through.

## 4. Compounding Engine

**Eyebrow**

Why It Compounds

**Headline**

**Body**

The value is not just in individual tools. It comes from the loop. OpenRecovery connects training to performance, resources to usage and outcomes, and engagement to action and follow-through. As more behavior moves through the system, recommendations improve, earlier signals emerge, and better outcomes become easier to repeat.

**Loop Statements**

Training -> performance

Resources -> usage -> outcomes

Engagement -> actions -> follow-through
